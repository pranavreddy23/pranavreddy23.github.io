Hi, I’m Pranav.
I like working on systems where you have to think about everything latency, safety, compute budgets, sensors, and how all the pieces behave under real constraints. I enjoy building pipelines end-to-end, scratching my head over timing mismatches, and digging under the hood to understand how a system actually works. Cyber-physical safety, performance, and sensor behavior are the things I care about the most.

I completed my Master’s in Computer Engineering at New York University, where I worked on robotics, planning, and learning-enabled control. Before that, I studied Electrical Engineering at SVNIT Surat, which built the foundation for all the systems work I do now.

Professionally, I’m a Research Engineer at MultiCoreWare, working across compute, sensors, and embedded systems. My work ranges from low-level driver debugging to radar/lidar modeling, GPU pipelines, server telemetry, and building dashboards for maintenance and remediation.

I’m also an active contributor, and one of the core members of the Autoware Foundation’s POV project. It’s one of my favorite things I work on, and if you’re curious, you can check out my GitHub or my Autoware contributions.

Outside of tech, I live for sports. There are very few games I haven't tried. Cricket, tennis, volleyball, you name it. I'm always ready for a match, and fortunate to pick things up quickly. Martin Scorsese movies and pizza practically defined my teenage years. You'll also catch me deep in a movie, reading up on geopolitics, deep tech, and anything that sparks curiosity.

If you want to chat about robotics, tech, or just want to share a pizza recommendation, feel free to reach out!
